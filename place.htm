History

Zimbabwe, formerly Rhodesia, gained independence in 1980 after a long struggle. The name Zimbabwe, meaning "great house of stone," references the ancient Great Zimbabwe archaeological site, a testament to the region's rich historical heritage.

Unique Wildlife

Zimbabwe boasts incredible biodiversity, with national parks like Hwange hosting large elephant populations, diverse bird species, and iconic African wildlife. The country is home to part of the magnificent Victoria Falls, one of the world's largest waterfalls.

Geography

Located in southern Africa, Zimbabwe features diverse landscapes including the highveld plateau, mountainous eastern highlands, and savanna plains. The country is landlocked, bordered by South Africa, Botswana, Zambia, and Mozambique.
